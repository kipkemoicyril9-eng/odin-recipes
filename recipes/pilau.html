pilau dish

Pilau is a fragrant, one-pot rice dish that is a staple in many households. It is famous for its rich brown color and the warm aroma of whole spices like cloves, cardamom, and cinnamon.

This recipe features tender chunks of beef and perfectly seasoned rice, making it a hearty meal often served during celebrations or special family gatherings.

ingridients

  • Rice: Basmati rice (soaked/washed)
  • Meat: Beef, chicken, or goat, often boiled until tender first.
  • Pilau Masala (Whole or Ground): A blend of cumin seeds, cardamom pods, black peppercorns, cinnamon sticks, and cloves.
  • Aromatics: Lots of red onions (thinly sliced), garlic, and ginger paste.
  • Liquid: Beef broth/stock (ideal) or water.
  • Fat: Cooking oil or ghee.
  • Optional/Optional: Potatoes (halved/quartered), tomato paste, and salt.
  1. prepare the meat : Boil the meat with salt, garlic, and ginger until tender. Separate the meat from the broth and set both aside.
  2. Sauté Onions:Heat oil/ghee in a large pot. Add onions and fry until they turn deep brown (this dictates the color of the pilau).
  3. Add Aromatics & Spices:Add the garlic-ginger paste and cook until fragrant. Stir in the pilau masala (whole or ground) and cook for another minute.
  4. Brown the Meat:Add the boiled meat to the spice mix, stirring well to coat and brown the meat, adding potatoes if desired.
  5. Add Rice & Liquid:Add the soaked, drained rice and stir for 2–3 minutes to toast. Pour in the reserved broth/water (about 2 cups water for 1 cup rice).
  6. Simmer & Steam:Bring to a boil, then cover with a tight lid (use foil for a better seal) and reduce heat to low. Cook for 15-20 minutes until the water is absorbed and the rice is fluffy.
  7. Serve:Serve hot with kachumbari (onion-tomato salad) and chili sauce.
