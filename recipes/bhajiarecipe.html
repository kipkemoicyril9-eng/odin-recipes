steps and ingridients to making a golden crisp bhajia

Move over, French fries—there’s a new favorite in town! Our Kenyan-style Coast Bhajias are all about that perfect 'crunch.'

Thinly sliced potatoes dipped in a vibrant, herbaceous gram flour batter and fried to perfection. It’s a burst of warm spices in every bite, making them the perfect companion for a cold drink and great conversation.

ingridients

  • Potatoes:4–5 large potatoes (Peeled and sliced into very thin, translucent circles)
  • Gram Flour (Besan):1.5 cups (This is chickpea flour; it’s what gives bhajias their unique taste).
  • All-purpose Flour:2 tablespoons (Adding a little wheat flour makes them extra crunchy).
  • Rice Flour (Optional):1 tablespoon (For an even louder "crunch").
  • Ginger & Garlic Paste:1 tablespoon (Fresh is best).
  • Turmeric Powder:½ teaspoon (For that iconic golden-yellow color).
  • Cumin Powder (Bizari ya mchuzi):1 teaspoon.
  • Red Chili Powder:½ teaspoon (Adjust based on your guests' spice tolerance).
  • Green Chilies:1–2, finely minced (Optional, for an extra kick).

steps

  1. Slice and Soak:Peel and slice potatoes into very thin, uniform circles, then soak them in cold salty water for 15 minutes to remove excess starch.
  2. Dry Thoroughly:Drain and pat the potato slices completely dry with a kitchen towel; if they are even slightly damp, the batter will slide off during frying.
  3. Create the Base:Whisk together gram flour (besan), a little all-purpose flour for crunch, turmeric for color, and your blend of cumin and chili powder.
  4. Add Fresh Aromatics:Stir in fresh ginger-garlic paste, a squeeze of lemon, and a generous amount of finely chopped dania (coriander) to give the batter its signature look and scent.
  5. Perfect the Consistency:Gradually add water to create a thick, velvety batter that coats the back of a spoon—then toss in the dried potatoes until every slice is fully covered.
  6. The Master Fry:Drop slices individually into medium-hot oil and fry until they are a vibrant golden-orange and crisp, then drain on a wire rack to maintain that crunch.

he best ingredient in any dish is the company you share it with. Serve these bhajias and samosas while they’re piping hot, add a generous squeeze of lemon, and watch them disappear! Happy cooking, and even happier eating."
