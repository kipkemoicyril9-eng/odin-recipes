samosa recipe

steps and ingridients to cook samosa

There is nothing quite like the aroma of fresh samosas frying in the kitchen. Whether you are serving them at a party or enjoying them with a hot cup of tea,

These triangular pastries are filled with a perfectly spiced mixture of meat or vegetables and fried to perfection for that iconic crunch."

ingridients
  • Minced meat (Beef):500g (Lean is best so they aren't too oily)
  • Onions:2 large (Finely diced—onions are the secret to flavor!)
  • Garlic & Ginger paste:1 tablespoon
  • Green chilies:1–2, finely chopped (optional)
  • Coriander (Dania):A generous handful, chopped
  • Spices:
    • 1 tsp Turmeric (for color)
    • 1 tsp Black pepper
    • Salt to taste
  • Squeeze of Lemon: To brighten the flavors.
    • steps to make samosa the clasic kenyan style
    1. Prep the Meat:Brown the minced beef in a dry pan until all moisture evaporates; dry meat ensures a crunchy (not soggy) samosa.
    2. The Flavor Secret:Stir in garlic, ginger, and spices, then turn off the heat before folding in raw diced onions and fresh coriander to keep them sweet and crunchy.
    3. Make the Dough:Knead a smooth flour and water dough, let it rest, then roll into thin "sandwiches" with oil and flour in between.
    4. Lightly Sear:Briefly heat the rolled dough on a dry pan for seconds to "set" the pastry, then peel the layers apart to create thin, professional wrappers.
    5. Fold and Seal:Shape the wrappers into cones, fill with the completely cooled meat mixture, and seal tightly with a thick flour-and-water "glue" paste.
    6. Fry Gold:Deep fry on medium heat, turning frequently until they are golden brown and puffed up

    serve hot with fresh lemon wedges!
